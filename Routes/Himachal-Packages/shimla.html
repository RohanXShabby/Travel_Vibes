Shimla
The Queen of Hills

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is a mesmerizing hill station nestled in the Himalayas at an altitude of 2,205 meters. It is renowned for its colonial architecture, lush green landscapes, and a cool, pleasant climate throughout the year. Once the summer capital of British India, Shimla retains its vintage charm with landmarks like the Viceregal Lodge, Christ Church, and the bustling Mall Road. The Ridge, an open space in the heart of the city, offers panoramic views of the surrounding snow-capped mountains. Tourists visit Shimla for its blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure activities. During winter, the town is blanketed in snow, making it a favorite destination for skiing and snow enthusiasts. Must-visit attractions include Kufri, Jakhoo Temple, Green Valley, and Mashobra. Whether you're seeking a peaceful retreat, thrilling adventures, or a glimpse of history, Shimla promises an unforgettable experience in the lap of nature.
