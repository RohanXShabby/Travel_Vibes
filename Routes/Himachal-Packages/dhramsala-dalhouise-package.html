Dharamshala & Dalhousie
The Abode of the Dalai Lama and Little Switzerland

Dharamshala and Dalhousie, nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, offer a unique blend of cultural richness, spiritual serenity, and breathtaking natural beauty. Dharamshala, known as the abode of the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government in exile, exudes a tranquil and spiritual atmosphere. Dalhousie, often referred to as "Little Switzerland," boasts colonial-era architecture, lush green hills, and stunning views. This combined destination offers a diverse experience, from exploring Tibetan culture and spirituality to enjoying the serene landscapes and colonial charm of Dalhousie.

Tour Overview

Day 1

Pathankot - Dharamshala

Arrive at Pathankot and transfer to Dharamshala (2 hours). Settle in and enjoy leisure time. Dharamshala is known as the functioning capital of the Tibetan government in exile. Dinner and overnight stay.

Day 2

Dharamshala

Explore Mcleodganj, the residence of the Dalai Lama. Visit the local bazaar, Namgyal Monastery, and St. John in the Wilderness Church. Dinner and overnight stay.

Day 3

Dharamshala - Dalhousie

Travel to Dalhousie (4 hours), a town with colonial architecture and scenic views. Check into your hotel and enjoy the surroundings. Dinner and overnight stay.

Day 4

Dalhousie - Khajjiar

Visit Khajjiar, known as "India's Switzerland," with its picturesque lake and surrounding forests. Explore the Kalatop-Khajjiar sanctuary. Dinner and overnight stay.

Day 5

Depart Dalhousie

After breakfast, check out and transfer to Pathankot railway station (2 hours) for your onward journey.
